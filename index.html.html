Future Vision: PhD Research Goals

Microfluidic "Organonanochip" Platform Development

Target Program: PhD in Biomedical Engineering at University of Oklahoma

Prospective Supervisor: Dr. Rupa Haldavnekar

My research vision centers on developing revolutionary microfluidic platforms that co-culture primary tumor cells, immune cells, and fibroblasts to recreate the earliest moments of tumorigenesis. By coupling this with in-chip Raman spectroscopy and self-functionalized nanoprobes, I aim to discover dynamic, interaction-based biomarkers that traditional static assays miss.

Key Research Objectives:

  • Design modular, multiplexed biosensor platforms for specific cancer subtypes
  • Integrate machine learning as an adaptive diagnostic "eye" within devices
  • Develop patient-specific markers through real-time cell-cell interaction analysis
  • Translate laboratory discoveries into robust clinical tools

This interdisciplinary approach combines my expertise in molecular biology, computational modeling, and bioengineering to make truly personalized cancer care a daily reality for patients worldwide.
